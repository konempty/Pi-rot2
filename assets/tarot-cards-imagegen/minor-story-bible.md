# Pi Tarot Minor Arcana Story Bible

All minor cards are inner illustrations only. The fixed template supplies the border, nameplate, English card title, and rank. No generated image should contain readable text, words, numbers, borders, or nameplates. Pi-inspired ornamental motifs are allowed when they fit the scene.

Detailed Ace~Ten timeline events now live in `assets/tarot-cards-imagegen/minor-number-storyboard.md`. Treat that storyboard as the source of truth for numbered minor cards. Page, Knight, Queen, and King are court cards, so they may work as symbolic role portraits rather than strict chronological episodes.

Shared visual rules:
- High-quality kawaii fantasy / anime-inspired tarot illustration.
- Purple, lavender, cream, and suit accents dominate.
- Gold is a restrained accent: coin charms, thin trim, readable golden ornaments, and modest clothing motifs are welcome.
- Avoid gold-dust or glitter-spray treatment across the whole image, and avoid tiny dense gold patterns scattered over the protagonist's clothing. Small clothing motifs are fine when they are readable and not noisy.
- Pi-inspired marks should be used intentionally. For Wands, Cups, and Swords inner illustrations, avoid round Pi coin badges, chest medallions, bag charms, or background logos unless a prompt explicitly asks for one; the fixed tarot frame already supplies the deck's Pi identity. For Pentacles, the suit object is a Pi coinseed: a round medallion with a warm gold rim, deep purple enamel face, and a clean gold Pi-inspired emblem.
- The Pi coinseed emblem should resemble the Pi Network coin/app icon, not a plain mathematical Greek pi character: two small square dots above, a thick continuous rounded bridge, two vertical stems below, and a short solid right arm attached to the right stem that turns upward. The right arm must stay attached and slim; it must not become a C-shaped loop, ring, circular hole, or swollen upper-right bulb. Make the motif larger or use fewer motifs if needed so the icon shape is readable; prefer clear larger coinseeds over many tiny symbols. It should feel like original fantasy ornamentation inspired by the Pi coin icon, not a flat exact official vector copy.
- Avoid excessive metallic gold, dense glitter, unreadable clutter, plain mathematical Greek pi glyphs, typed pi symbols, T-shaped coin marks, plus-sign coin marks, malformed two-oval smiley coins, plain/no-emblem coins, Roman numeral II coin marks, two standalone vertical-bar coin marks, straight-horizontal-cap coin marks, C-shaped right loops, circular right-side holes, swollen right-top bulbs, or many tiny repeated Pi marks.
- For minor cards, keep the protagonist visually central. Faceless hands, arms, or partial bodies do not count against the character limit, and a small number of supporting villagers, helpers, or messengers is allowed when it makes the scene more natural. Avoid crowded scenes that steal focus.
- Numbered minor cards must make the suit-object count readable at card scale. If the card is Four of Wands, exactly four wands must be visually obvious; if Seven of Cups, seven cups must be obvious, and so on.

## Wands Story: The Ember Trail

Consistent cast and setting: a youthful spark apprentice with short dark wavy hair, violet eyes, a purple travel cloak, cream tunic, orange scarf, star satchel, and a small cream fox-like companion. The story takes place along a lavender mountain trail, ember academy, and warm village festival. Wands are refined magical staffs or ritual rods with gold bands, violet crystals, and soft orange flame, not mundane lumber or chopped wood.

Arc: discovers the spark wand, plans the journey, looks outward, celebrates with the village, trains with friends, receives recognition, defends the hill, sends swift messages, guards through fatigue, magically bears responsibility, then matures through Page, Knight, Queen, and King aspects of creative fire.

## Cups Story: The Pearlwater Village

Consistent cast and setting: a gentle young cup keeper with soft lavender-brown hair, pearl hairpin, purple-and-cream seaside clothes, and a tiny water-sprite companion. The story takes place around a moonlit pearl pond, seaside garden, fountain courtyard, and quiet shore. Cups are pearl-white vessels with aqua glow.

Arc: receives the overflowing cup, forms a bond, celebrates friendship, turns inward, grieves softly, remembers kindness, faces dream choices, leaves for deeper meaning, finds contentment, shares family joy, then matures through Page, Knight, Queen, and King aspects of emotional wisdom.

## Swords Story: The Moonwind Academy

Consistent cast and setting: a clever young sky student with silver-black bobbed hair, violet academy cloak, cream scarf, and a small owl-like wind familiar. The story takes place in a moonlit academy, misty shore, windy hill, quiet chapel room, and river crossing. Swords are elegant silver practice blades with blue wind light.

Arc: gains clarity, balances a choice, feels heartbreak and healing, rests, faces conflict, crosses to calmer thought, uses strategy, recognizes limitation, sits with anxiety, reaches an ending and dawn, then matures through Page, Knight, Queen, and King aspects of intellect.

## Pentacles Story: The Coinseed Garden

Consistent cast and setting: a grounded young coin gardener-merchant with warm brown hair, green-violet apron, cream shirt, and a small sprout mascot. The story takes place in a terraced coinseed garden, open-air garden worktables, village market, and harvest greenhouse. Pentacles are purple-enamel Pi coinseeds with warm gold rims, grown among leaves, soil, stone, and careful garden tools.

Arc: plants the first coinseed, balances resources, learns careful cultivation, protects value, endures scarcity, practices generosity, waits for growth, masters garden work, enjoys abundance, builds legacy, then matures through Page, Knight, Queen, and King aspects of material care.
